1. Essential Core Concepts
Before writing code, you must understand how industrial hardware operates:
* Electrical Circuits: Basic understanding of voltage, current, and continuity.
* Series vs. Parallel: How components link together to create or interrupt a path.
* Relays & Switches: How physical switches control power flow to a load.
* Schematic Reading: The ability to read standard electrical blueprints.

2. Digital Logic Foundations
Ladder logic executes based on true/false states. You need a grasp of:
* Binary States: Understanding 0 (Off/False) and 1 (On/True).
* Boolean Gates: Familiarity with AND (series), OR (parallel), and NOT (inversion) logic operations.

3. Basic Hardware Familiarity
You should know what a Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) is and its key hardware components:
* Inputs: Push buttons, proximity sensors, and limit switches.
* Outputs: Motors, valves, indicator lights, and solenoids.
* CPU Scan Cycle: How a controller reads inputs, executes logic, and updates outputs sequentially.
